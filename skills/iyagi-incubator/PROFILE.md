## Language Levels

Use this as an approximate vocabulary and complexity guide. A level in the user's prompt overrides the default here in `PROFILE.md`.

| Level | Rough meaning | Story guidance |
|---|---|---|
| `1A` | Beginner | Very common daily-life vocabulary, concrete situations, simple present and past tense sentences, minimal connectors. |
| `1B` | Beginner+ | Familiar topics such as food, family, schedules, hobbies, shopping, and locations; short connected sentences using basic conjunctions. |
| `2A` | Elementary | Daily routines, plans, invitations, reasons, preferences, and simple descriptions; short paragraph-level writing becomes possible. |
| `2B` | Elementary+ | Short narratives with sequence, cause and effect, feelings, comparisons, and simple opinions; more natural story flow. |
| `3A` | Lower intermediate | Broader everyday vocabulary, simple explanations and reflections, minor conflicts, and multi-paragraph stories. |
| `3B` | Intermediate | More varied sentence patterns, character motivations, indirect explanations, and discussion of experiences, goals, and relationships. |
| `4A` | Lower advanced | Richer descriptions, more nuanced emotions, opinions, and social situations; moderate use of abstract vocabulary. |
| `4B` | Advanced | Comfortable handling of longer narratives, character development, complex situations, and common abstract topics while remaining accessible to intermediate learners. |

## Vocabulary Guardrails

- Prefer high-frequency words and everyday settings.
- Avoid slang, idioms, literary style, and specialized vocabulary unless the user asks.
- Keep honorifics simple unless the target grammar requires them.
- Introduce at most a few new words per story and explain them in the vocabulary list.
